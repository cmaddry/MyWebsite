JILA: Research Assistant

Over the last three years, I have been building and calibrating magnetic tweezers. Magnetic tweezers are a scientific instrument used to manipulate and study biomolecules, such as DNA or proteins, by applying controlled magnetic forces. They work by using an external magnetic field to exert force on superparamagnetic beads attached to the target molecules. This technique enables us to investigate mechanical properties such as elasticity and binding interactions. Our magnetic tweezers use a high-speed CMOS camera capable of tracking micron-sized particles at over 2000 frames per second. Calibration is performed by measuring the elasticity of double-stranded DNA, a well-known constant.
